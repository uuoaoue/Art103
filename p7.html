Phase 4: Story Sign off and Rough Draft of Model

Suppositions and Backstory
In a world where the sun is twice as close to the earth as it is now.

The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System. It is a nearly perfect ball of hot plasma, heated to incandescence by nuclear fusion reactions in its core, radiating the energy mainly as visible light, ultraviolet light, and infrared radiation. It is by far the most important source of energy for life on Earth.

Currently the distance between the earth and the Sun is around 93 million miles. What happens if the sun is now only 46 million miles far from the earth?

In theory, the sun is getting more intense with all the lights and heat and the earth will be an incompatible place to live in. Earth will experience higher temperatures, heat waves, etc.

However, because of gravitational force, by the time the sun gets closer to the earth, it, in fact, uses almost all of its energy. Therefore, human beings will receive less light and energy from the sun. ( the sun is getting weaker so it got pulled closer to the earth). As a result, the earth actually does not have enough energy to survive. We, at that time, need some artificial light/ heat to keep us continuously warm and help us produce more lights for the earth.
