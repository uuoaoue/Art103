FINAL PRODUCT

Artist Statement

Terraforming is one of the components of our project that we address, and it's genuinely intended to complement the overarching concept of building your unique individual alien world. Although terraforming in practice includes altering the atmosphere, temperature, and other internal environment characteristics to represent Earth-like life, we chose to concentrate on some of the more topographical parts of the process.

Our design package includes elements such as height stands and geographical feature variations that can simulate an unlimited number of imagined ecologies by adjusting surface topology. We encourage everybody working with our design set to explore how their landforms might help to mimic viable habitats for plants and aliens alike and how the ecologies they construct can influence the stories they choose to tell. Our goal is to put the tools in the builder's hands to satisfy their own proposals about the ideal world they see and act as a medium for world-building in general.

I think what was successful about this project is that we were able to come up with the idea that allows the pieces to be interchangeable. It was hard at the beginning when we did not know where to start with the project. Also, the ground in the original idea was somehow symmetric and could not create depth and height to the overall project. I think being able to develop the concept to a level that we can change the elevation of the base is what makes it interesting. For future project, I'd document things while we are doing it so that it can save lots of time garthering the images for the documentation and make the documenting process less time-consuming.
